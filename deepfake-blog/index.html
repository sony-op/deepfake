Don't let AI fool you. Learn to detect the lies.

Imagine receiving a video call from your father asking for money urgently because he's in the hospital. You panic and send the money. Later, you find out he was at home sleeping. This is not a movie sceneтАФthis is a Deepfake Scam, and it's happening right now in India.

1. What Exactly is a Deepfake?

Deepfake technology uses Artificial Intelligence (AI) to replace a person's likeness with someone else's. It uses a method called "GANs" (Generative Adversarial Networks).

Think of it like a digital mask. Scammers feed thousands of photos of a target (like a celebrity or your family member) into a computer, and the computer learns how to mimic their face and voice perfectly.

2. 5 Advanced Ways to Spot a Fake

AI is smart, but it's not perfect. Physics and biology are hard to fake. Look for these signs:

  • ЁЯСБя╕П The Blinking Test Real humans blink naturally (every 2-10 seconds). Deepfakes often stare uncomfortably long without blinking, or they blink in a weird, fluttery way.
  • ЁЯСД Lip-Sync & Teeth Look closely at the mouth. Does the audio match the lip movements perfectly? Also, AI struggles to render individual teethтАФthey often look like a solid white bar.
  • ЁЯзг Skin Texture & Lighting Real skin has pores and wrinkles. Deepfake skin often looks "too smooth" or plastic-like. Also, check the shadowsтАФif the sun is on the left, the shadow must be on the right. AI often messes this up.
  • ЁЯСУ The "Glasses" Glitch If the person is wearing glasses, watch the frames. In deepfakes, the glare on the glasses often doesn't move correctly when the head moves.
  • ЁЯФК Audio Quality If it's a voice clone, listen for robotic pauses or a lack of emotional breathing. The voice might sound "flat."

тЪая╕П ALERT: The "Virtual Kidnapping" Voice Scam

Scammers can clone a voice from just 3 seconds of audio (from Instagram Stories). They call parents, play a crying voice of their child, and demand ransom.

Immediate Action Plan:

  1. Do Not Panic: Fear is their weapon.
  2. Cut the Call: Hang up immediately.
  3. Verify: Call your child/relative on their real number.
  4. Ask the Secret Question: Ask something only they would know (e.g., "What is the name of our old dog?").

3. Legal Action in India (IT Act)

Creating or distributing malicious deepfakes is a crime under Section 66D of the Information Technology Act, 2000 (Cheating by personation using computer resources).
