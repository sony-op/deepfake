1. What Exactly is Deepfake Technology?

Deepfake is a combination of "Deep Learning" and "Fake". It relies on a specific type of Artificial Intelligence called GANs (Generative Adversarial Networks).

Think of GANs as two competing robots: one robot (the Forger) creates a fake image, and the second robot (the Detective) tries to spot the difference. They repeat this process millions of times until the fake image is so perfect that the Detective robot can't tell it apart from the real one.

  • ЁЯСД Lip-Sync & Teeth Rendering Look closely at the mouth. Does the audio match the lip movements perfectly, or is there a slight delay? Furthermore, AI struggles to render individual teeth. In many fakes, the teeth appear as a solid white bar.
  • ЁЯзг Skin Texture & Lighting Physics Real skin has pores and wrinkles. Deepfake skin often looks "too smooth" or plastic-like. Also, check the lighting consistency. If the sun is on the left, the shadow must be on the right.
  • ЁЯСУ The "Glasses" Glitch If the person is wearing glasses, watch the frames. In deepfakes, the glare on the glasses often doesn't move correctly when the head turns.
  • ЁЯФК Audio Quality (Voice Cloning) Listen for "robotic" pauses or a lack of emotional breathing. Deepfake audio often sounds "flat."
